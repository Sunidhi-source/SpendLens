# User Interviews

## Interview 1 — R.K., Co-founder / CTO, B2B SaaS (seed, 8 people), May 2026

**Context:**
Cold DM on X. R.K. had recently posted about their API bill going up 3x in two months. 12-minute call over Google Meet.

**What they said:**

- "I don't know what changed. We didn't ship anything — the models just got more expensive or someone on the team is hammering something."
- "I've looked at our Cursor invoice three times and I still don't know if Business was the right call versus just buying more Pro seats."
- "Honestly, if it just told me 'you're fine' and I could trust it, that's worth something too."

**What surprised me:**
He said the tool he was most likely to act on a recommendation for was Cursor, not OpenAI — because the Cursor billing page is confusing and he had never been sure he was on the right tier. I had assumed API spend would be the biggest pain point.

**What I changed after this:**
Added a clearer per-tool explanation of _why_ a plan is or isn't right for team size, not just the savings number. R.K. needed justification, not just a cheaper number.

## Interview 2 — P.S., Engineering Manager, Series A (35 people), May 2026

**Context:**
Posted a "quick 10-min chat" ask in the Indie Hackers Slack. P.S. responded within an hour. 14-minute call.

**What they said:**

- "The problem is I don't control the AI budget directly — finance does. So I need something I can forward to them."
- "If it said 'Credex' in the first sentence I'd close it. I don't want to be sold to."
- "I'd share the public link if the numbers were good, because it makes me look like I'm paying attention."

**What surprised me:**
He said the shareable report was the most valuable feature — not for going viral, but for forwarding internally to a CFO. He wanted to be able to say "here's the analysis" rather than "here's my spreadsheet." I hadn't thought of the share URL as an internal document.

**What I changed after this:**
Moved the Credex CTA deeper into the results page so it only appears after the full breakdown. Made the public report URL more prominent with a short explanation: "Share this link — identifying details are stripped."

## Interview 3 — A.M., Solo founder, developer tools (pre-revenue), May 2026

**Context:**
Cold DM on Indie Hackers. A.M. runs a small AI coding assistant and is highly cost-sensitive. 11-minute async voice note exchange.

**What they said:**

- "I'm on basically every free tier. So this might just tell me I'm doing everything right."
- "Actually honest, which is unusual. Most of these things manufacture savings to make you feel like you need to change."
- "I'd share it with my Slack community just because it's a clean tool."

**What surprised me:**
She immediately said she'd share it, but specifically because the tool was honest enough to say "you're spending well" for a lean stack — not because the savings were impressive. The low-savings verdict was the feature.

**What I changed after this:**
Made the "You're spending well" message significantly more prominent for low-savings audits. It had been a small muted line. Now it's a full headline, because the honest result is the most shareable one.
