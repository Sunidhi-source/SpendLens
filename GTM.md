# GTM

The exact target user is a technical founder or VP Engineering at a seed to Series A startup with 5-75 employees, at least three engineers, and monthly AI spend that has grown from “a few subscriptions” into a messy mix of coding agents, ChatGPT/Claude seats, and API usage. They are not trying to run procurement like a large enterprise; they just want someone to tell them which invoices are wasteful without slowing the team down.

Right before wanting this tool, they are likely searching or scrolling around “Cursor team plan cost,” “Claude Team minimum seats,” “OpenAI API bill too high,” “GitHub Copilot Business worth it,” or founder posts about burn reduction. The emotional trigger is not curiosity. It is the monthly close, a runway update, or a surprise API invoice.

Specific channels: Hacker News “Show HN,” r/SaaS, r/startups, r/LocalLLaMA for API-cost discussions, Indie Hackers, the YC founder Slack if accessible through alumni networks, AI Engineer Discord, Latent Space Discord, Cursor community Discord, and X lists around seed-stage CTOs and AI tooling operators. I would also comment on live threads where founders complain about AI bills, but with a concrete mini-audit instead of a generic link drop.

First 100 users with $0 paid budget: day 1, publish a transparent teardown of a sample AI stack and post it on X/LinkedIn with the exact savings math. Day 2, DM 30 founders/operators with a personalized opening line about one tool they publicly use. Day 3, post a “roast my AI bill” thread and manually audit the first 10 replies. Day 4, ship a short benchmark table from anonymized audits. Day 5, post to HN as a working tool, not a launch essay. Day 6-7, ask every completed audit user to share the public result URL.

The unfair channel for Credex is proprietary knowledge of discounted AI credits. A generic calculator can say “downgrade this plan”; Credex can say “keep the same workflow, but stop paying retail here.” Week-1 traction that suggests this works: 100 completed audits, 25 captured emails, 8 high-savings leads, 3 booked calls, and at least one founder publicly sharing a report.
